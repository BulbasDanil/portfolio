Projects

MR Chess Assistant

In Spring 2025 Meta released the Passthrough API for their headsets, opening new possibilities for mixed reality applications.

This allowed me to merge my interests in MR, Computer Vision, and chess into a single app, while testing the potential of the new API.

As a result I created an MR chess assistant capable of detecting the board state and overlaying hints directly in the player’s view.

The app flow is the following:

  • Captures a real-time image from the headset
  • Sends it to a server that detects pieces and maps them to FEN format
  • Server queries chess‑api.com to get the best move and sends it back
  • App maps the move from 2D image space to 3D world space
  • Virtual piece is overlayed over the real board showing the suggested move

It’s a perfect substitute for other less comfy assisting devices—though you won’t go unnoticed wearing a Quest. Hopefully, next‑gen AR glasses will fix that.

Peaceful Valley


Hackathon winning mixed-reality environmental experience


At a Hackabull 2025, my team set out to build a mixed-reality storytelling experience designed to raise environmental awareness.

We wanted to deliver impact not through technical complexity, but by emphasizing artistic expression and emotional engagement.

To achieve this, we created a game where visuals, narrative, music, and sound effects worked together to immerse players, prioritizing atmosphere and cohesion over complex mechanics to create a powerful “wow” effect.

eXploRe a Wonderland of Inquiry

Educational VR game developed for middle school students.

Our client commissioned our team to build Alice in Wonderland VR, a game for 8th-grade students that combined immersive storytelling with logic and problem-solving challenges, with the final goal of showcasing at AWE 2025.

I led development of the boat-building level, where I engineered physics-based boat assembly with snap points and constraints, implemented paddle locomotion with stroke detection, and developed resizing effects to scale the player in VR.

Full Playthrough ( ~4 min)

The project consists of three main parts:

  • Intro level with interactive menu
  • Hallway scene, acting as a hub between levels
  • Boat building level, challenging kids to build a boat to cross the river

The client gave us flexibility with mechanics, allowing us to experiment with innovative VR interactions. The project was structured around three parts: an intro level with an interactive menu, a hallway hub scene, and a boat-building level.

The resizing mechanic proved the most difficult, as I had to mitigate motion sickness caused by rapid scale changes. I solved this with FOV vignetting and gradual scale interpolation, ensuring comfort while preserving immersion.

Despite the physically demanding testing process, the final experience was smooth and reliable. The project was later presented at AWE 2025, helped secure over $50,000 in grant funding, and was deployed in a school camp program.

Lateral Flow Strip Quantifier

Over Summer 2025 joined an interdisciplinary startup project MegaNano BioTech led by immunologist Dr. Chuanhai Cao, who developed rapid tests for detecting Alzheimer’s biomarkers.

I led a team of three developers to solve this by building the computer vision algorithm and creating an image-processing application that transformed subjective line intensity into objective data through optical density analysis.

I also built a React-based web interface where users could upload images and instantly receive clear, quantitative results.

The project is ongoing, with plans to expand the app to support multiple test types and integrate machine learning for enhanced accuracy and usability.

Egmont Key AR Tour

Mobile XR tour guide developed for Egmont Key State Park, an island near Tampa known for its wildlife and historical ruins. My first commercial project.

The task was to create an app that would help visitors navigate the island using Bluetooth trackers and visualize the historical appearance of virtually restored buildings through augmented reality.

I designed and implemented the core functionality, including the integration of Bluetooth beacons for navigation and the AR features for overlaying reconstructed structures on their real-world locations.

While the project was not the most complex in terms of design or technical execution, it marked my introduction to XR development and gave me hands-on experience building an application that blended history, technology, and user interaction in a real-world setting.
